RULES OF PLAY (Digital Game)

NUMBER OF PLAYERS: 2

OBJECT OF GAME: Each player is a Zoo Director on assignment to capture 3 wild animals for transport to the Zoo. 1st to complete an assignment wins.

TO BEGIN GAME

  1. You and the computer both have a continent and animals relevant to that geographic region to capture.
  2. You will answer questions about animal habits, trail signs, methods of capture, and standard tronsport to the Zoo.

GENERAL INSTRUCTIONS

  1. ANIMALS ARE TRACKED AND CAPTURED ONE AT A TIME, in the order they appear on the screeen. AFTER EACH ANIMAL IS CAPTURED IT MUST BE TRANSPORTED TO THE ZOO before you can track the next animal down.
  2. TRACKING: Each animal appears twice on the trail. You have a choice of Trails and may track the animal in either location.
  3. MOVES ARE MADE BY: Following instructions in the central panel OR using the Spinner.
  4. SPINNER: When you land on an "S" CIRCLE, ON YOUR NEXT TURN use the Spinner. If the "S" Circle is RED, advance the number of spaces indicated on the RED Section of the Spinner. If the "S" Circle is GREEN, advance the number of spaces indicated on the GREEN Section of the Spinner.

METHOD OF PLAY

  1. PREPARATION FOR TRAIL: Circles 1-2-3 on the Zoo Parade Board require YES or NO answers. Follow the instructions that appear in the central panel. EXAMPLE: If your Answer is Correct-the Computer will Advance You to Circle 2;If incorrect--There is no move and you must answer Question 1 again ON YOUR NEXT TERM.
  2. ADVANCEMENT: When you answer Question 2 correctly, the Computer will Advance you to Circle 3. Then you are ON THE TRAIL.
  3. ON TRAIL: Locate the 2 positions of the animal you are tracking. You have a choice of Trails and may track the animal in whichever direction you choose.
    • TRAIL SIGNS: When you land on a small green circle, ON YOUR NEXT TURN you will be prompted with ON TRAIL questions, which include Pictures. If the Trail Sign indicates the nearby presence of your animal -- answer YES. If not -- answer NO.
    • PASSING YOUR ANIMAL ON TRAIL: When your move takes you BEYOND the animal you are tracking, it has been frightened from the area and escapes to its other locations on teh Trail. ON YOUR NEXT TURN you must start to track the animal in its new location.

      • NOTE: If the computer moves you back to the spot from which the animal fled, remember tha tit is no longer there. ON YOUR NEXT TURN you will be prompted with the Spinner and will advance the number of spaces indicated in the RED Section.

    • LANDING ON OTHER ANIMALS: When your move puts you on the "C" Circle of an animal you are NOT tracking, the computer will IMMEDIATELY advance you 2 spaces out of range.
    • "H" CIRCLES ARE HAZARDS: When you land on an "H" Circle, Ithe computer will IMMEDIATELY give you instructions.
    • "C" CIRCLES: (1) When you land on the "C" CIRCLE of the animal you are tracking, the computer will refer you IMMEDIATELY to to methods of Capture. State your choice and follow instructions. (2) When the central panel indicates CAPTURED --the computer will remove your Marker from the Board. ON YOUR NEXT TURN you will transport animal to the Zoo. If the animal escaped, the computer will indicate this and move you, and you will attempt to capture ON YOUR NEXT TURN.
  4. TRANSPORT TO ZOO: You will be prompted with 3 methods of transport --Air, Ship, and Rail. If you select the usual method used by a Zoo Director, you will successfully have transported your animal to the Zoo. If you selected a method of transfer not usually used, you must select another method on your next turn.
  5. THE WINNER: 1st to transport 3 assigned animals to the Zoo wins.

ABOUT THE PROJECT

The instructions above and most of the gameplay is based on the original MARLIN PERKINS' ZOO PARADE game from 1954.

Professor and Historian Daniel Bender, author of "The Animal Game: Searching for Wildness at the American Zoo"
